Best Children's Books

by Ann Healy,
on April 9, 2017

Reading to the youth of today is one of the most important things one can do. Reading will aid in intellectual development and can be fun in the process. Starting to read to your children while they are young will inspire them to dream. To believe that they can be whatever they what to be when they grown up. Reading can also instill lifelong values that you want your children to learn that will transform them into a generation of caring, loving adults. Here are some of the best children's books that will put a smile on everyone's face.

Dr. Seuss

Anything by Dr. Seuss is bound to be a big hit. Each of this author's well renowned books is chocked full of laughter. However, each has a hint of meaning or morality in every tale. For example, "Oh, The Places You Will Go!" is a book about literally that, all the places in the world you can go. How you can be anything, see the world if you want to, all it takes is determination and the drive to do so. Then of course, children and adults alike love the tale of "The Grinch Who Stole Christmas." This hilarious story about Mr. Grinch teaches everyone to have more compassion for people in this world. The best thing about his books is that they can be read to various age groups.

Where The Wild Things Are

This book was written in 1973, however its age has not meant that this classic tale has lost its touch with children. This story is about a young child named Max who uses his wild, creative imagination to transcend his boring life. He creates his own fairy-tale life where he romps though the jungle with his monster friends. Except, Max does this all from the comfort of his own bed. This is a literary tale about a child who is feeling sad and lonely. It can help you teach your children to have strength and courage when times get tough.

Charlotte's Web

This classic literally tale teaches two very important lessons. That keeping honest and true is always the best course to take. Also, everyone needs to have a good friend in their life. Friends make growing up more fun and interesting. Also, Wilbur teaches us that even as the runt of his liter, he wants to be loved. Each child deserves to grow up in a loving and happy family. Without kindess, how can a child ever learrn to love the world, or themselves? This book will teach the children in your family how to grow together, love together, and how to be a strong family unit.

Good Night Moon

This is a perfect book to read the little ones before bed. It is educational through the use of a bedtime story. Your young child is learning that there is indeed a moon. You can elaborate more depending on their age about the moon and stars for a more compelling learning experience. As you read, you are also teaching that the moon really does have to "sleep" so the sun can shine.
