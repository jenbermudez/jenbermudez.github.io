Best toy for boys age 10-12

by Dan Jones,
on April 9, 2017

Yes, the old school bicycles and lego sets are still available. Oldies never die. In my 30s now, I can recall having endless amounts of fun. Hours and hours of outdoor playing and activities with nothing but a bicycle. The neighborhood kids and I would just ride around for hours and hours. During the summertime, when it was light until almost eight or even nine o’clock, our mom's would yell out their front doors for us to “Get our butts inside and wash up,” for either dinner or bed. Young boys these days need to have those experiences. I know they can't have the same exact ones that the neighborhood boys and I did, but something similar is all I would want for my boy growing up. That's why one of the best toys for boys ages 10-12 in my mind is undoubtedly a bicycle.

Bicycle

The 24” Mongoose Ledge 2.1 Boys’ Mountain Bike is a great choice. This bike is great for young boys. I remember having a similar one when I was a kid. I absolutely fell in love with this bike when I woke up one snowy Christmas morning and there it was with a blue bow on the handlebars. I can almost feel the excitement I had that day as I reminisce about it now. The joy of a shiny new bicycle to a boy that age is overwhelming. This particular model comes in a silver/red two-tone color scheme. Made from aluminum, the full suspension and fork suppress bumps when cranking along at full steam. A Shimano rear derailleur with a 21-speed grip shift makes shifting on the fly a breeze. Handlebar Alloy linear brakes stop when needed. A three piece crank gives good gear support while aluminum alloy rims give lightweight strength. I find a bike like this to absolutely be one of the best toys out there for a boy ten to twelve years old. The main positive is the amount of exercise a boy will receive if he owns and loves a bike like this such as I did. As well as exercise, many other benefits come with a bicycle. Balance is another. Riding a bike takes balance most kids have no time learning. Once learned, that balance will help them later in life. Learning to ride takes patience, a skill most children these days need to find more of. Perseverance. Sticking it out until the riding is mastered is a life skill that will be taken away from this one experience and used over and over again throughout a lifetime. Last but not least is value. Learning to take care of a belonging like a bike is crucial to a child's understanding of appreciation. Things that are not respected are not taken care of and are often broken or lost. Having a bike like this is a great way to teach a young boy the value of a dollar and how to treat their own property so it lasts for years and years to come.
