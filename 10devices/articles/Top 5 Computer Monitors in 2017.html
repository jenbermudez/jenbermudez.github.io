Top 5 Computer Monitors in 2017

by Reesie Hofstadir,
on April 9, 2017

No doubt, computers are now a very essential part of every household. Whether you use it for graphics or text editing, online streaming, gaming, business software, freelance work, or just for everyday use, you’d surely want the best specs, features, hardware, and of course, computer monitor that would suit your needs. Some of our techie friends recommend the following computer monitor brands – in NO particular order – they think are ideal for everyone’s needs and yes, demands, too.

1. BenQ Ultra HD 4K Design Monitor

This sleek yet pretty affordable monitor is perfect for graphic designers, photographers and video editors. BenQ Ultra HD display uses premier color technology, delivers vivid, bright, crisp,and accurate color that will satisfy graphic design pros and artists alike. Housing an impressive10-bit 100 percent sRGB color palette, it also has In-Plane Switching (IPS) technology that lets you view angles and see bolder colors by allowing more light to pass through the panels. This enables a slight glow around the edges and corners, which are extraordinarily helpful for attention to detail.

2. Dell Ultrasharp-U2417HJ 23.8

This computer display brand never ceases to amaze its users and fans. Aside from improving picture quality and pixel density, this latest flagship from Dell adds an innovative wireless charging pad at the base of the monitor. It makes a lot of sense since more and more people are fond of charging their smartphones while sitting in front of their computers. This 2017 U2417HJ is a great full HD monitor with 60 Hz refresh rate, 1920 x 1080 resolution, not to mention its ports for different visual and audio functions. Brace yourself, what’s more admirable about this monitor is that it also pivots, tilts, and swivels. This lets you find that perfect angle on which to work on your projects, surf in the couch, watch movies or anything else.

3. Samsung-U28E590D 28-Inch U.HD. LED Monitor

Offering resolutions of up 3840 x 2160 – four times higher than that of full HD – at 60 hertz, this latest generation Samsung display supports a palette of one billion colors. This basically explains how your graphics, favorite movies, and games are projected on screen in a natural, meticulous yet realistic manner. This model includes its “Eye Saver Mode” option that generally reduces eyestrain. This is achieved through the reduction of flickers and blue light emissions, allowing you to surf the web, play games, watch movies, or browse your social media accounts for longer periods more comfortably than ever.

4. HP Pavilion 22cwaIPS LED Backlit Monitor

On a tight budget but still want to upgrade your monitor? Fret no more, HP Pavilion 22cwa gives you a good alternative. This 21.5-inch ultra-slim display provides its users high-quality video and picture output, thanks to the full HD 1080p-resolution, ultra-wide IPS technology, and LED backlighting. Dynamic contrast is in charge of its rich colors and ananti-glare treatment. There is also a thin bezel that keeps your eye on the display. Getting an average 4.5-star rating, this brand never fails to amaze its loyal clients for coming up with a display that gives you quality, modern vibe, and of course, value for money.

5. LG Electronics 34-Inch 34UC97-S WQHD I.P.S Curved Monitor

Ideal for HD movie marathons and prolonged use of computer, the slight curve of this LG monitor, as well as its large screen, aids in creating a very immersive viewing experience. This quality computer display offers sRBG-coverage over 99 percent for full, bold, and rich colors along with its two 7w internal speakers with MaxxAudio technology for a surround-like sound, thanks to its deeper bass as well as crystal clear highs that ensure superb audio quality. Compatible with both Windows and Mac, LG curved monitor also houses a wide range of preset modes, including cinema, photo, and gaming.
